The Federal Open Market Committee decided today to raise its target for the federal funds rate by 25 basis points to 4 percent. 

The disruptive effects of recent hurricanes seem likely to be temporary, especially in light of increased spending associated with rebuilding efforts. Economic growth continues to be supported by robust underlying growth in productivity. Core inflation and longer-term inflation expectations remain contained. However, high energy and other costs have boosted near-term inflation expectations and price pressures, likely making further policy firming necessary. 

The Committee perceives that, with appropriate monetary policy action, the upside and downside risks to the attainment of both sustainable growth and price stability should be kept roughly equal.
