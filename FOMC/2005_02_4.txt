The Federal Open Market Committee decided today to raise its target for the federal funds rate by 50 basis points to 2 3$4 percent. 

The Committee believes that the stance of monetary policy remains accommodative and, coupled with the underlying growth in productivity, is providing ongoing support to economic activity. Output appears to be growing at a moderate pace despite the rise in energy prices, and labor market conditions continue to improve. Inflation and inflation expectations remain well contained, but rising business costs have the potential to put upward pressure on prices. 

 The Committee perceives the upside and downside risks to the attainment of both sustainable growth and price stability for the next few quarters to be roughly equal.
